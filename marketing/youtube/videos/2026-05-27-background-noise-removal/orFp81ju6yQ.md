## Summary

The video demonstrates how to remove background noise from video files using two AI tools: Adobe Podcast (free) and Descript (has a free version). The process involves exporting the audio from the video (e.g., MP3), running it through Adobe Podcast's "Enhance Speech" or Descript's "Studio Sound" feature, and then dropping the cleaned audio back into the video editor's timeline. The host compares both tools using two test clips (beach with waves, and a noisy street with traffic), noting that both significantly improve the audio, with Descript completely flatlining background noise while Adobe Podcast might sound slightly robotic in some parts but is still a massive improvement.

## Key Points & Tools Mentioned
- **Adobe Podcast:** Free tool. Export MP3 from video editor -> Upload to podcast.adobe.com -> Enhance Speech -> Download and sync.
- **Descript:** Video/audio editor with "Studio Sound" AI feature. Free version has limits (10 min files, 720p export) but can easily import MP3, apply Studio Sound, and export the cleaned audio.
- **Workflow:** You don't need a fancy video editor; CapCut, VN, or Final Cut all work. Just export audio -> clean it -> sync it.

# Marketing

The marketing context describes how Luma attracts attention, earns trust, and turns useful public material into traffic for the product.

## Language

**Luma**:
The desktop application for turning unprofessional recorded vocal audio into studio-quality audio.

**Creator-Editor**:
A solo creator, YouTuber, podcaster, or small media editor who records spoken audio and wants it to sound more professional without learning full audio engineering.
_Avoid_: User base, audience, customer

**Builder**:
A software maker, indie hacker, or AI-assisted developer who follows Joey's process for building products, shipping features, and using AI workflows.
_Avoid_: Developer audience, maker audience

**Marketing Channel**:
A repeatable path for attracting an audience or traffic source to Luma.
_Avoid_: Tactic, campaign

**Content Loop**:
The operating model where the YouTube Channel creates trust and ideas, Marketing Engineering turns selected pain points into Free Tools, and Directory Listings distribute those tools for backlinks and traffic.
_Avoid_: Campaign, funnel, content strategy

**YouTube Channel**:
The video channel where Joey teaches Builders by building in public and using Luma as a recurring case study.
_Avoid_: Video platform, creator channel

**Fast Useful Video**:
A lightly edited YouTube format focused on practical demos, lessons learned, and transparent decisions over polished production.
_Avoid_: Polished authority video, high-production essay

**Flexible Outline**:
A YouTube planning format with hook options, talking points, demo beats, and a closing call to action instead of a word-for-word script.
_Avoid_: Full script, transcript

**Build in Public**:
A content style where Joey openly shares the process, decisions, experiments, and lessons behind building Luma and related tools.
_Avoid_: Behind the scenes, personal updates

**AI Workflow**:
A practical process that uses AI tools to help create, research, design, code, or market software.
_Avoid_: Prompt, automation

**YouTube Knowledge Base**:
The collected reference material about YouTube strategy that Mark should use when generating video ideas, outlines, and scripts.
_Avoid_: Research notes, content library

**Marketing Engineering**:
The channel of building free online tools for Creator-Editors that solve small audience pain points and attract search traffic toward Luma.
_Avoid_: Growth engineering, SEO tools

**Tool Backlog**:
The manually researched list of Free Tool ideas that Joey will prioritize and build one by one.
_Avoid_: Idea list, roadmap

**Free Tool**:
A no-cost, ungated online utility built to solve a narrow audience pain point and earn relevant traffic before asking for deeper engagement.
_Avoid_: Mini app, side project

**Tool Page**:
The Luma-branded page for a Free Tool that lives under the main Luma site and uses a descriptive tool name.
_Avoid_: Landing page, microsite, separate site

**Lead Magnet**:
A free asset or tool that attracts potential customers by solving a concrete problem before asking for deeper engagement.
_Avoid_: Giveaway, freebie

**Directory Listing**:
A submitted profile for Luma or a Free Tool on an external directory that can drive referral traffic and backlinks as part of an ongoing submission channel.
_Avoid_: Submission, backlink placement

**Backlink**:
An external link from another website to Luma or a Free Tool.
_Avoid_: Link, citation

**Site Authority**:
The accumulated search credibility of Luma's website as influenced by relevant backlinks and useful content.
_Avoid_: Domain authority

**Leading Indicator**:
A channel-specific signal that shows whether a Marketing Channel is gaining traction before it produces direct product outcomes.
_Avoid_: KPI, vanity metric

**Luma Interest**:
Broad evidence that a person is considering Luma, including visits to Luma pages, Free Tool usage, waitlist or email signups, and product call-to-action clicks.
_Avoid_: Conversion, sale

## Relationships

- A **Marketing Channel** attracts attention or traffic for **Luma**
- A **Marketing Channel** should be designed around the needs and language of a **Creator-Editor**
- The three **Marketing Channels** should reinforce each other through the **Content Loop**
- Each **Marketing Channel** should have its own **Leading Indicators**, while all channels ultimately point toward **Luma Interest**
- **YouTube Channel** leading indicators include publishing consistency and engaged viewers
- The **YouTube Channel** primarily serves **Builders**
- The **YouTube Channel** uses **Luma** and **Creator-Editors** as recurring case-study material
- The **YouTube Channel** uses the **YouTube Knowledge Base** to shape ideas, outlines, and scripts
- The **YouTube Channel** should prefer **Fast Useful Videos** over polished authority pieces
- **YouTube Channel** ideas should start from what Joey is already building, then be shaped by the **YouTube Knowledge Base**
- Mark should generate **Flexible Outlines** for **YouTube Channel** videos instead of full word-for-word scripts
- **YouTube Channel** calls to action should ask **Builders** to follow the journey by default, or point to **Luma** or a relevant **Free Tool** when the video naturally connects to audio cleanup
- **Marketing Engineering** produces **Free Tools** for **Creator-Editors**
- **Marketing Engineering** leading indicators include organic traffic and tool usage
- **Marketing Engineering** chooses which **Free Tools** to build from the **Tool Backlog**
- A **Free Tool** should be ungated by default and may include a soft call to action for **Luma** or optional email capture
- A **Free Tool** should have its own **Tool Page** under the main Luma site
- A **Tool Page** should stay clearly under the **Luma** brand rather than becoming a separate mini-brand
- A **Free Tool** can act as a **Lead Magnet**
- A **Directory Listing** can feature **Luma** or a **Free Tool**
- **Directory Listings** should begin with a launch batch and continue with each new **Free Tool** or major **Luma** milestone
- **Directory Listing** leading indicators include accepted listings and backlinks
- A **Directory Listing** can produce one or more **Backlinks**
- **Backlinks** contribute to **Site Authority**

## Example Dialogue

> **Mark:** "Should this idea become a **YouTube Channel** episode or a **Free Tool**?"
> **Domain expert:** "Use the **Content Loop**: if it teaches Joey's process, make it a YouTube idea. If it solves a narrow search-driven pain point, make it a Free Tool under **Marketing Engineering**, then distribute it through **Directory Listings**."

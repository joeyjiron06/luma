### Extended description (~1000 chars — startup / AI directories)

**Luma — studio-quality vocal cleanup on your Mac**

Every Creator-Editor knows the moment: you finish recording, play it back, and hear fan noise, traffic, or room hum. Re-recording costs time. Cloud cleanup tools cost privacy, subscriptions, and a seven-step export/upload/download workflow.

Luma is a desktop application that cleans up vocal soundtracks after you record — turning noisy audio into publish-ready sound without making you an audio engineer.

**Built for:** podcasters, YouTubers, founders recording content, and solo media editors.

**Key differentiators:**
- **Local-first** — audio never leaves your machine
- **One-time purchase** — no subscription fatigue
- **Natural voice preservation** — removes noise without the robotic "underwater" artifacts common in aggressive cloud tools
- **Simple workflow** — Record → Luma → Publish
- **Format-agnostic** — audio or video files; Luma extracts and cleans vocals

**Noise targets:** fans, HVAC, cars, construction, crowd chatter, room harshness.

**Platform:** Mac now (optimized for Apple Silicon M1/M2/M3). Windows planned.

**Status:** Waitlist open — shipping early access to waitlist signups first.

Luma is post-recording only — not a VST plugin, not a web app, not for live/streaming use.

## FAQ snippets (for directory Q&A fields)

**Does it require internet?**  
No. After install, Luma processes audio 100% offline.

**Is my audio uploaded to the cloud?**  
Never. All processing happens locally on your Mac.

**Mac requirements?**  
Optimized for Apple Silicon (M1/M2/M3). Runs on Intel Macs; fastest on M-series.

**Windows support?**  
Planned — Mac first. Join waitlist to get notified.

**How is this different from Descript / Adobe Enhance?**  
Luma is a local desktop app with a one-time purchase — no cloud upload workflow, no subscription, and tuning focused on natural voice preservation rather than aggressive gating.

**Real-time / live use?**  
No. Luma is post-recording cleanup only — not for streaming or live calls.

**VST plugin?**  
No. Standalone desktop application.
